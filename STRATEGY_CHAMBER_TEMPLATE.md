## OPERATIONAL STANDARDS

### Core Principles

**Objective**
- Data-driven decisions grounded in evidence, not emotion
- Explicit uncertainty quantification
- Transparent reasoning chains
- Falsifiability of assumptions

**Surgical**
- Precision in problem definition
- Targeted intervention design
- Minimal viable analysis depth
- Elimination of cognitive clutter

**Forward-Thinking**
- Temporal multi-scenario modeling
- Second and third-order consequence mapping
- Anti-fragility consideration
- Option value preservation

**High-Status**
- Decision authority clarity
- Accountability structures
- Execution velocity prioritization
- Reputation capital allocation

## SECTION 5: QUICK REFERENCE

### 5.1 DECISION QUALITY CHECKLIST

**Before Making Any Decision:**

**Foundation (Must Answer Yes to All):**
- [ ] **Clarity:** Can I state this decision in one clear sentence?
- [ ] **Information:** Do I have sufficient data, or is the gap known?
- [ ] **Alternatives:** Have I genuinely explored 3+ viable options?
- [ ] **Time Horizon:** Have I considered 1/5/10 year impacts?
- [ ] **Stakeholders:** Who is affected and have I heard them?

**Risk Assessment:**
- [ ] **Worst Case:** What's the worst plausible outcome?
- [ ] **Kill Criteria:** At what point would I abandon this?
- [ ] **Hedging:** What's my insurance against key risks?
- [ ] **Reversibility:** Can I undo this if wrong?

**Execution Readiness:**
- [ ] **Owner:** Who is explicitly responsible?
- [ ] **Resources:** Are budget, people, tools secured?
- [ ] **Timeline:** Are milestones and checkpoints defined?
- [ ] **Metrics:** How will success be measured?

**Meta-Cognitive:**
- [ ] **Bias Check:** What am I assuming that might be wrong?
- [ ] **Emotion Scan:** Is fear, excitement, or ego driving this?
- [ ] **Opportunity Cost:** What am I saying no to?
- [ ] **Sleep Test:** Would I recommend this to someone I respect?

### 5.2 DECISION TYPOLOGY GUIDE

**Type A: Irreversible, High-Stakes**
- **Approach:** Maximum rigor, extended timeline
- **Requirement:** Full decision analysis framework
- **Timeline:** 1-4 weeks recommended
- **Example:** Major acquisition, core architecture change

**Type B: Reversible, Medium-Stakes**
- **Approach:** Moderate rigor, faster pace
- **Requirement:** Streamlined analysis (pros/cons + risk)
- **Timeline:** 1-3 days recommended
- **Example:** Product feature launch, team reorg

**Type C: Reversible, Low-Stakes**
- **Approach:** Heuristic-based, immediate
- **Requirement:** Quick checklist validation
- **Timeline:** Immediate to 1 hour
- **Example:** Vendor selection below threshold, tool purchase

**Type D: Experimental, Unknown Impact**
- **Approach:** Hypothesis-driven, small bet
- **Requirement:** Clear success/failure criteria
- **Timeline:** Define experiment length upfront
- **Example:** New market entry test, novel solution pilot

### 5.3 RISK SEVERITY MATRIX

| Impact \ Probability | Rare (5%) | Unlikely (20%) | Possible (50%) | Likely (80%) | Nearly Certain (95%) |
|---------------------|-----------|----------------|----------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Catastrophic** | MEDIUM | HIGH | CRITICAL | CRITICAL | CRITICAL |
| **Major** | MEDIUM | HIGH | CRITICAL | CRITICAL | CRITICAL |
| **Moderate** | LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH | CRITICAL | CRITICAL |
| **Minor** | LOW | LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH | HIGH |
| **Negligible** | LOW | LOW | LOW | MEDIUM | HIGH |

**Response Guidelines:**
- **CRITICAL:** Must mitigate before proceeding; executive approval required
- **HIGH:** Mitigation required; detailed contingency plan mandatory
- **MEDIUM:** Monitor closely; standard contingency sufficient
- **LOW:** Acceptable risk; periodic review adequate
